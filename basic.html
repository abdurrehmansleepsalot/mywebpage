Phone & DSLR

  • Then cameras evolved from mechanical mechanisms to digital formats. The use of SD-cards was such a revolutionary change, as the reel had the possibility of being damaged, but SD cards were a safer way.
  • Even today, DSLRs are being used, and their advanced form, mirrorless cameras, are even more advanced than DSLRs.
  • The most compact way to photograph moments is with phones. Nowadays, every phone comes with a camera. Although the quality is not as great as that of DSLRs, it can get the job done easily. Moreover, phones are not exactly camera-centric, but things can be improved. As it is said, it is more about the skills of the cameraman.

Format to shoot with

Basically, there are two formats to work with:

jpeg
  1. Simplest format to shoot on
  2. Takes less space
  3. Requires no professional exposure
  4. Is not flexible to work with
 raw
  1. Complex format to shoot on
  2. Occupies more space
  3. Requires some exposure to camera settings
  4. Very flexible to work with

So, by this comparison, we can see how JPEG differs from RAW. If you are a beginner, I would recommend working with JPEG, but as you gain experience, the switch to RAW format becomes very necessary.


Angles

Once you have selected the format and device to work with, the next point to consider is the angles you're going to work with. There are several to choose from, such as:

  • Angles of thirds
  • Subject in the center
  • Golden rule

Start to shoot

All of this information is enough to start with. Now you are all set to go and start shooting pictures. If you are using a phone, just open the camera app and start shooting. If you are using a DSLR, just use auto. See, the most important thing is whether you are working or not. That is what matters. If you have started, you will obviously improve over time and become a better photographer. But the thing that matters is start now!
